## Purpose

As a UW Bothell engineering student, securing competitive internships and entrylevel positions requires standing out in a crowded applicant pool. One of the
primary barriers to landing an interview is a resume that fails to quickly
communicate value, even if your underlying technical abilities are strong. This
document outlines structural best practices to help you optimize your resume,
translate your academic and personal technical experiences into industry-aligned
achievements, and match the scanning habits of engineering recruiters.

--
## How Recruiters View Resumes: Understanding the Visual Priorities


Most recruiters do not read every line of an engineering resume during the
initial review phase. Instead, they scan the document in a matter of seconds to
check if your background satisfies basic job specifications. Understanding where
their focus lands allows you to place your strongest technical highlights
precisely where they look first.

tools, or engineering methodologies highlighted in the job posting. Detail your

verify your design files.

## Use Clear Action-Oriented Language

### Why Formatting and Word Choice Impact Scan Time

Engineering recruiters scan profiles rapidly and lose focus when encountering
passive phrases or vague framing like "was responsible for" or "assisted with."
These phrasing styles hide your actual contribution and slow down the
recruiter's ability to assess your competence.

### How to Use Effective Action Verbs

Begin every single bullet point on your resume with a concise, direct action
verb that details your exact engineering input. Utilize definitive words such as
"designed," "implemented," "tested," "simulated," or "debugged." This structure
forces you to write clear, impactful sentences that communicate technical skill
immediately.

## Quantify Technical Impact

### Why Numbers Matter to Engineering Recruiters

Listing basic engineering actions only proves that you can follow step-by-step
instructions. To show that you understand the performance implications of your
design choices, you must provide clear results. Numbers and percentages show
recruiters that you are focused on optimization and efficiency.

### How to Add Data to Your Achievements

Include measurable outcomes, performance metrics, and technical results within
your experience summaries. For example, instead of writing "worked on a circuit
project," frame the achievement as "designed a circuit that improved signal
efficiency by 20% or cut system power consumption."

## Maintain Clean and Simple Formatting

### Why Simple Layouts Avoid ATS Filtering Issues

Many large engineering firms utilize automated Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS)
to filter resumes before a human ever views them. Overly complex templates with
multi-column formats, text boxes, or embedded graphics frequently cause these
scanning programs to misread your engineering data, leading to automatic
rejections.

### How to Organize Your Page Design

Structure your resume using a clean, single-page, single-column layout with
standard fonts and uniform margins. Keep your formatting consistent across all
sections, and separate entries using simple bullet points to make the page
readable.

## Leverage Campus Engineering Resources

### Why Independent Review Falls Short

Developing a strong engineering resume is an iterative process that requires
external feedback. Reviewing your own work often causes you to overlook minor
formatting inconsistencies, technical jargon errors, or structural clarity
issues that a professional would catch instantly.

### Where to Find Help on Campus

Schedule an appointment with the UW Bothell Writing and Communication Center
(WaCC) or connect with advisors through the MyUWB portal. You can find
specialized resume guides, templates, and career workshops on the [UW Bothell
Career Services resume platform](https://www.uwb.edu/career/resumes).
